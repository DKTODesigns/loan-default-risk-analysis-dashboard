Project Overview

Understanding
Lending Risk at Scale

This project examines a large-scale loan portfolio to uncover the behavioral, geographic, and financial patterns that drive default risk. Starting from raw data, the analysis follows a complete analytics pipeline — from cleaning and exploration to modeling and interactive visualization.

Using a dataset of over 112,000 loan applications across four regions, the project identifies which borrower segments, loan types, and economic conditions correlate most strongly with default outcomes.

  • Data cleaning, transformation, and feature engineering
  • Exploratory Data Analysis with outlier detection
  • Multivariate statistical analysis and correlation mapping
  • Predictive risk modeling with scikit-learn
  • Interactive filtering dashboard with Dash & Plotly
  • Business intelligence recommendations for lending strategy

Key Insights

What the Data Reveals

Six evidence-backed findings from the exploratory and statistical analysis phases.

01
🌍
Regional Risk Varies Significantly
The North-East region shows the highest average default rate (~30%) in the EDA, while the interactive dashboard reveals the South region with the highest observed default activity. Both findings confirm that a blanket national lending policy is insufficient — localized underwriting thresholds are essential.
02
🎓
Education Loans Default Most Often
Education-purpose loans carry a 33% default probability — the highest of any loan purpose — followed by Purchase loans at 26%. Refinance loans, by contrast, show the lowest default rate at 23%.
03
🏦
FHA: Accessibility vs. Risk Trade-Off
FHA loans represent a small share of the portfolio (13.8%) yet carry a 34% default rate versus 23% for Conventional. This tension between lending accessibility and portfolio risk requires stricter FHA-specific assessment criteria rather than broad portfolio-wide adjustments.
04
📊
Credit Score Weakly Predicts Default
Despite intuition, credit score alone is a weak discriminator — defaulted and paid-off loans overlap widely across the 500–900 range, suggesting that other variables (DTI, loan purpose, region) carry more predictive weight.
05
📅
Mid-Year Loan Volume Peak
Monthly loan volume peaks at ~$4.8B in June before declining steadily through year-end, reaching $3.3B in December. This seasonal pattern implies capacity planning and risk concentration windows for lenders.
06
🔍
Property Value Has Outlier Problem
Property value shows over 2,200 statistical outliers — far more than any other feature — skewing regression lines significantly. Robust preprocessing or winsorization is essential before modeling collateral-based risk.

Strategic Recommendations

Actionable Insights
for Lenders

Evidence-driven recommendations derived from the analysis to reduce portfolio default risk.

01
Enhance Underwriting with Multi-Variable Scoring
Shift from credit-score-dominant decisions to comprehensive multi-variable risk scoring models that incorporate DTI ratio, property value, loan purpose, region, and interest rate spreads simultaneously.
02
Apply Targeted Assessment by Loan Segment
Apply stricter review criteria and adjusted rate thresholds for FHA loans and higher-risk loan purposes (Education, Home Improvement). Blanket national policies underperform compared to segment-specific underwriting.
03
Implement Localized Regional Risk Monitoring
Implement localized risk monitoring with adjusted underwriting policies for high-default regions. Regional underwriters should factor local economic conditions into risk scoring rather than applying uniform national thresholds.
04
Proactively Track Leading Risk Indicators
Track interest rate spreads and debt-to-income ratios systematically as leading indicators of portfolio stress. These metrics signal emerging risk earlier than credit scores, enabling preemptive intervention.
05
Deploy Regularized Predictive Models
Integrate Ridge-regularized regression models (alpha=0.1, polynomial features) into the daily borrower evaluation and pricing workflow. Train/test validation (85/15 split) confirmed robust R² generalization performance.
06
Expand Dashboard to Forward-Looking Forecasting
Expand dashboard capabilities beyond historical exploration to include real-time portfolio monitoring and forward-looking default forecasting, transforming the tool from a reporting instrument into a proactive risk-management engine.
